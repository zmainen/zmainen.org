Zach Mainen

Lisbon, Portugal

I am a Principal Investigator at the Champalimaud Centre for the Unknown in Lisbon, where I run a systems neuroscience lab studying serotonin, decision-making, and perception. I co-founded the International Brain Laboratory, a collaboration rethinking how neuroscience is done. I also write on artificial intelligence, formal ontology, and the design of institutions, and founded Latent States, a research programme exploring subjective experience through science and art.

Research

Systems Neuroscience Lab
mainenlab.org
Systems neuroscience of serotonin, decision-making, and rapid perception. Neural circuits underlying confidence, uncertainty, and flexible behavior in mice and humans.
Latent States
latentstates.org
Situated experiments at the intersection of neuroscience, art, and subjective experience. Festival research, immersive installations, and collaborative work across perception, consciousness, and embodied cognition.
