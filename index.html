Zach Mainen

Neuroscientist · AI Researcher · Champalimaud Foundation, Lisbon

I am a Principal Investigator at the Champalimaud Centre for the Unknown in Lisbon, where I run a systems neuroscience lab studying serotonin, decision-making, and perception. I co-founded the International Brain Laboratory, a collaboration rethinking how neuroscience is done. I also lead HAAK, a research project at the intersection of AI, formal ontology, and governance, and Latent States, an art-science practice.

Research

Neuroscience Lab
mainenlab.org
Systems neuroscience of serotonin, decision-making, and rapid perception. Neural circuits underlying confidence, uncertainty, and flexible behavior in mice and humans.
AI & Governance
haak.world
Formal ontology for AI systems. Constitutional governance of autonomous agents. The Library Theorem and externalized intelligence. Open infrastructure for collective human action.
Art Practice
latentstates.org
Latent States: art-science installations exploring perception, agency, and the boundary between observer and observed. Collaborative work across neuroscience, music, and visual art.
